# English Writing Style Guide

## Basic Principles

### 1. Academic Tone
- Use third person (avoid "we", "our")
- Objective statements, avoid speculation
- Quantitative over qualitative descriptions

### 2. Terminology
- First mention: full term (abbreviation)
- Subsequent: abbreviation only
- Example: acetylcholinesterase (AChE) → AChE

### 3. Numbers and Units
- Arabic numerals: 10 mg/kg, 25°C
- Unit spacing: 10 mg (with space), 10% (no space)
- Ranges: 10-20 mg/kg or 10 to 20 mg/kg

## Sentence Structures

### Introduction Three-Part Structure
```
[Context] The diamondback moth (Plutella xylostella) is a major pest of cruciferous crops, causing over $5 billion in annual losses globally.
[Problem] Prolonged reliance on chemical control has led to widespread insecticide resistance.
[Gap] However, a systematic review of neuropharmacological targets in P. xylostella remains lacking.
```

### Technical Section Loop
```
[Mechanism] Nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) are critical targets in insect nervous systems.
[Insecticides] Neonicotinoids act as nAChR agonists, causing neuronal hyperexcitation.
[Resistance] Target-site mutations (e.g., R81T) and metabolic detoxification (CYP6CM1 overexpression) confer resistance.
[Implications] Non-competitive modulators may overcome target-site resistance.
```

## Common Expressions

### Evidence Strength
- Strong: studies demonstrate, has been established, clearly shows
- Moderate: studies suggest, results indicate, data support
- Weak: preliminary evidence, early studies, may indicate

### Causality
- Direct: causes, leads to, results in
- Correlation: is associated with, correlates with, accompanies
- Speculation: may, suggests, implies

### Comparison
- Similarity: similarly, likewise, consistent with
- Contrast: however, in contrast, unlike
- Progression: furthermore, moreover, more importantly

## Expressions to Avoid

### Informal Language
- ❌ a lot of studies → ✅ numerous studies
- ❌ very important → ✅ critical
- ❌ pretty good → ✅ effective

### Vague Expressions
- ❌ some → ✅ 15 studies
- ❌ recently → ✅ between 2020 and 2025
- ❌ significant → ✅ significant (P < 0.05)

### Absolute Terms
- ❌ always, never, completely
- ✅ typically, generally, in most cases
